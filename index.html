Some purchases were made of course. They had a Space Marines themed backpack that I happily added to my collection. I wanted something a little more stylish to carry my models in for paint club. Additionally, I purchased the latest issue of White Dwarf magazine since it had a bunch of redemption codes for retro 40k games on gog.com. That's a steal! Aside from the purchases, Warhammer World had tons of diaromas, models painted from WarhammerTV (shoutout to Duncan Rhodes), lots of tables to play on, a demo area for model painting, and a freaking cafe! All of the drinks were obviously Warhammer themed. I got the hexwraith glow drink. It was a green apple lemonade with a caramel in it. Heavenly, and shockingly cheap. Like $2.50 for the drink! If you ever find yourself in Texas, run, don't walk, to Warhammer World!

December 4, 2022

11:48 A.M.

Last night I finally finished reading Bloodlines. I was thoroughly impressed with some of the twists and turns of that book. In my opinion, the best warhammer novels are those that push against the stereotypical characteristics of the archetypes laid out in the base lore of the 40K universe. Characters that question whether or not the Empoeror of Mankind portrayed by the Ministorium is the right way to worship their lord. Having villains that do terrible things for a cause other than money is a welcomed bonus. I'd highly recommend that anyone read Bloodlines. It's simply excellent. Side note, there is a short passage on page 205 that had me doubled over in laughter. Zidarov is looking around a supply station and notes all of the various mauls the room. Something about needing to mention every variation or accessory to mauls was hilarious to me.
